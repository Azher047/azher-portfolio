Automated Valuation Engine — 73 Strings

Project Details

Project Type: Company

Company Name: 73 Strings

Total Duration: 9 months

My Role: PM, Valuation Product & Strategy Lead

Key Technologies

NLP & ML Graph Databases Vector Similarity Data Pipelines UX/Figma

Overview

At 73 Strings, I led product development for an Automated Valuation Engine that transformed how PE/VC investors, LPs, and valuation teams perform CCA, CCM, income-based valuations, and scenario analysis. The system combined big-data ingestion, NLP, and network-graph intelligence, processing $10M$ datapoints monthly to deliver fast, consistent, and audit-ready valuation outputs.

The Problem

Private-market valuation was slow, manual, and inconsistent. Analysts spent hours sourcing comps, cleaning datasets, and justifying assumptions. Data was fragmented, comp selection was subjective, and audit trails was weak. The industry needed a repeatable, transparent, and data-driven valuation workflow.

My Role

  • PM owning valuation modules, similarity logic, and UX.
  • Partnered with data science & engineering on models and pipelines.
  • Conducted user research with PE/VC valuation teams.
  • Defined scoring logic, thresholds, and comp-recommendation framework.
  • Oversaw ingestion of $10M$ monthly datapoints across filings, reports & web sources.

Approach

The core innovation centered on moving beyond simple industry codes to capture genuine business similarity using a knowledge graph.

  1. Unified Data Infrastructure: Integrated data from 10-Ks, industry reports, M&A databases, and unstructured web sources using NLP and entity resolution.
  2. Company Knowledge Graph: Built a graph capturing industries, revenue models, growth patterns, investor relationships, and product/market attributes for more intelligent comp relevance.
  3. Comp Recommendation Engine: Used vector similarity, ratio clustering, graph proximity, and financial filters to automatically identify high-quality CCA and CCM comps.
  4. Automated Valuation Modules:
    • CCA: auto-filtered comps, removed outliers, generated weighted multiples.
    • CCM: surfaced relevant transactions with deal context.
    • Income Approach: streamlined DCF with WACC suggestions.
    • Scenario Analysis: downside/base/upside sensitivities.
  5. Analyst-Focused UX: A clear valuation workspace with editable assumptions, override options, and full auditability.

Impact

  • $10x$ times faster valuation turnaround.
  • Consistency across teams using standardized models.
  • Improved comp accuracy with graph-based matching.
  • Scalability: $10M$ datapoints processed monthly.
  • Audit-ready outputs increasing trust & transparency.

Key Learnings

  • Explainable AI drives adoption.
  • High-quality comp selection is a data problem, not a modeling problem.
  • NLP + graph intelligence meaningfully outperform static industry codes.
  • UX familiarity is critical for analyst adoption.

Earnings Call Intelligence — 73 Strings

Project Details

Project Type: Company

Company Name: 73 Strings

Total Duration: 6 months

My Role: PM, NLP Pipeline & Taxonomy

Key Technologies

BERT Model NLP & ML Sentiment Analysis Taxonomy Design

Overview

At 73 Strings, I led product development for an Earnings Call Intelligence system that applied advanced NLP to quarterly earnings call transcripts. The platform extracted entities, classified management commentary into investor-relevant themes, and analyzed sentiment shifts—allowing analysts to understand a company’s outlook in minutes instead of hours.

The Problem

Earnings calls are dense, unstructured, and time-consuming to analyze:

  • Calls run 60–90 minutes, with hundreds of lines of dialogue.
  • Analysts must manually highlight tone changes, risks, and strategy updates.
  • Traditional keyword search fails to capture context or sentiment.
  • Insight quality varies significantly across analysts.
  • Teams struggle to track what changed quarter over quarter.

Investors needed a way to instantly surface the key signals buried in each earnings call.

My Role

  • PM managing the NLP pipeline, domain taxonomy, and insight logic.
  • Conducted research with PE, public equities professionals to define what matters in earnings calls.
  • Collaborated with ML engineers to design BERT-based extraction models.
  • Defined classification buckets, sentiment scoring, and trend-detection rules.
  • Designed an analyst-friendly UI for surfacing insights quickly.

Approach

  1. Built a BERT-Powered Earnings Call Pipeline

    The NLP system handled:

    • Entity extraction: competitors, products, geographies, metrics.
    • Section detection: prepared remarks vs. Q&A.
    • Sentiment scoring: contextual, sentence-level sentiment.
    • Tone-shift tracking: detecting caution, optimism, hedging language.
  2. Classified Management Commentary into Investor Themes

    We created a domain-specific taxonomy aligned with how analysts evaluate businesses:

    • Revenue & demand signals.
    • Margin and cost drivers.
    • Operational performance.
    • Competitive updates.
    • Product and market expansion.
    • Guidance, risks & management uncertainty.

    This allowed us to summarize the call into theme-based insights, not raw text.

  3. Highlighted What Changed This Quarter

    A key innovation was quarter-over-quarter comparison:

    • New risks or uncertainties introduced.
    • Changes in tone around revenue, margins, guidance.
    • Emerging competitive threats.
    • Shifts in management confidence.

    This turned the product into a tool for narrative trend analysis, not just text parsing.

  4. Delivered a Real-Time Analyst Workflow

    The final interface included:

    • Theme-level summaries with sentiment bars.
    • “Top risks & opportunities” extracted automatically.
    • Key quotes and supporting context.
    • Q&A insights (often the most revealing part).
    • One-click export for valuation memos or internal reporting.

    Analysts could digest a full earnings call in under one minute.

Impact

  • Hours $\rightarrow$ minutes for earnings call analysis.
  • Standardized insight extraction across teams.
  • Improved accuracy by using contextual NLP over keyword tools.
  • Enabled faster quarterly reporting cycles.
  • Supported better trend identification across portfolio companies.
  • Reinforced 73 Strings’ positioning as an AI-driven valuation & intelligence platform.

Key Learnings

  • The Q&A section contains the most valuable, least structured insight.
  • Tone shifts matter more than absolute sentiment.
  • Sector-specific taxonomies outperform generic NLP models.
  • Analysts need explainable insights—transparency drives adoption.
  • Context windows in earnings calls are critical for accurate classification.

Excel Add-In for Market & Valuation Analysis — Gain.pro

Project Details

Project Type: Company

Company Name: Gain.pro

Total Duration: 6 months

My Role: PM, Workflow Automation & Integration

Key Technologies

Excel Add-in SDK API Integration Authentication (SSO) Data Caching UX/Ribbon Design

Overview

At Gain.pro, I led product development for an Excel Add-In designed to bring verified company intelligence, financial data, and screening capabilities directly into the workflows of private equity investors, investment bankers, and corporate development teams. The plugin enabled users to pull Gain.pro data straight into Excel models, automate comps, and build faster market/valuation analyses—all without switching tools.

The Problem

Despite modern intelligence platforms, analysts still run their core financial analysis inside Excel:

  • Valuations, comparables, and deal models all live in spreadsheets.
  • Pulling company data from different sources is slow and error-prone.
  • Analysts manually copy/paste data from platforms into Excel.
  • Data becomes outdated quickly, requiring constant refreshes.
  • Teams need consistent, audit-ready numbers across models.

Users wanted to work where they already spend their time—Excel—without losing Gain.pro’s depth and accuracy.

My Role

  • PM owning product vision, feature definition, and integration logic.
  • Ran discovery sessions with PE, IB, and CorpDev analysts to understand workflows.
  • Designed the functions library (company lookup, financials, KPIs, comps, filters).
  • Worked with engineers on API endpoints, data caching, and authentication flow.
  • Partnered with UX to design a lightweight ribbon and intuitive formula builder.
  • Established data validation and consistency rules across models.

Approach

  1. Mapped Analyst Workflows Inside Excel

    We observed real investment teams using:

    • CCA/CCM models.
    • Commercial due diligence models.
    • Market sizing sheets.
    • Growth benchmarking frameworks.

    This shaped the feature set to match actual usage patterns, not theoretical ones.

  2. Integrated with Core Gain.pro APIs

    We set up:

    • Secure authentication (SSO + API keys).
    • Caching layers for faster refresh cycles.
    • Server-side validation to ensure consistent numbers vs. the platform.
    • Usage analytics to track formula adoption.

    This ensured accuracy and reliability for deal teams.

  3. Designed an Analyst-Friendly Ribbon UI

    The ribbon included:

    • A formula builder for non-technical users.
    • Pre-built templates (CCA, CCM, screening, market maps).
    • A smart search bar for company lookup.
    • One-click data refresh.

    The goal: zero learning curve.

Impact

  • Analysts saved hours per week on data gathering.
  • Eliminated manual copy/paste errors and outdated data.
  • CCA/CCM workflows became significantly faster.
  • Teams built more consistent valuation and deal models.
  • Increased use of Gain.pro data inside due-diligence deliverables.
  • Strengthened Gain.pro’s stickiness in analyst workflows.

Key Learnings

  • Excel is still the “source of truth” for financial users—meeting them there drives adoption.
  • Custom formulas must be transparent and editable, not black boxes.
  • Speed matters; analysts abandon tools that slow down models.
  • Templates accelerate onboarding more than documentation ever will.
  • Accuracy and consistency are the primary trust drivers for IB and PE users.

Real-Time Digital Ad Strategy — Hackathon Project

Project Details

Project Type: Personal/Hackathon

Total Duration: 48 hours

My Role: PM, Design, & Full Stack POC

Project Link:

Key Technologies

GPT Analysis Browser Agents JSON Schema Real-Time Data

Overview

In a 48-hour product hackathon at Gain.pro, I built a proof-of-concept platform that automates competitive ad research for marketers. Instead of manually tracking ads across Meta, Google, or LinkedIn and piecing insights together, the solution uses browser agents, structured GPT analysis, and a real-time dashboard to surface messaging themes, top ads, industry trends, and targeted recommendations instantly.

The Problem

Marketers spend hours every week collecting competitor ads manually:

  • Switching between Meta, Google, and LinkedIn ad libraries.
  • Taking screenshots or copying text into ChatGPT.
  • Losing visual context and dealing with unstructured output.
  • No real-time view of competitor activity.

With $6.5M+$ marketers globally and $10M+$ Facebook advertisers, competitive ad intelligence is critical—but the current workflow is fragmented, repetitive, and slow. Most marketers get stuck in data collection mode instead of strategy development.

My Role

  • Led product design, data workflows, and insight structure.
  • Designed browser-agent scraping logic (Meta $\rightarrow$ competitors $\rightarrow$ variants).
  • Built the GPT-powered analysis engine with structured outputs.
  • Designed and built the unified dashboard for ads + insights.
  • Created evaluation logic for “top ads” and industry trends.
  • Delivered the final demo at the hackathon.

Approach

  1. Automated Browser Agents for Scraping

    The system automatically captures:

    • Ad creative (headline, body, CTA).
    • Platform context.
    • Competitor identity.
    • Variants and placements.
    • Recency and activity window.

    Ads are instantly categorized by competitor for real-time monitoring.

  2. GPT-Based Structured Analysis

    The AI layer performs:

    • Message & tone analysis.
    • Audience inference.
    • Theme detection across a competitor’s portfolio.
    • Ranking of top ads per competitor.
    • Cross-competitor synthesis (industry themes).
    • Custom recommendations for the user’s own brand.

    All outputs follow a schema-first JSON format to prevent drift.

  3. Unified Insights Dashboard

    One interface shows:

    • Real ads with visuals.
    • Status of scrapers.
    • AI-generated insights.
    • Competitor comparisons.
    • Industry-level patterns.
    • Recommendations.

    Designed for fast strategy alignment in weekly marketing rituals.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: No Ground-Truth Performance Data

Public ad libraries don’t show actual performance metrics.

Solution:

  • Created a transparent proxy scoring model combining: Recency, Creative longevity (days active), Number of variants, Placement coverage, Engagement signals (when available), and Brand reuse patterns.
  • Weights are editable in the UI for explainability.

Challenge: GPT Drift & Hallucinations

Early versions invented audiences or changed output structure.

Solution:

  • Schema-first prompting (JSON + Pydantic validation).
  • Grounding via structured ad text only.
  • Prompt versioning.
  • Regression tests on a mini dataset to ensure consistent insights.

Impact

  • Hours of manual work reduced to seconds.
  • Reliable, repeatable, structured insights.
  • Real-time competitor monitoring.
  • Clear foundation for a production-grade ad intelligence tool.
  • Strong positive reception at the hackathon.

Key Learnings

  • Marketers need explainability more than automation.
  • Schema-first design is essential for stable AI outputs.
  • Proxy scoring can outperform raw ad-library browsing for strategic insights.
  • Real-time visibility drives better creative iteration cycles.
